## WORLD & TONE SNAPSHOT
- **Source Material:** "KochiKame" (Police Comedy Manga)
- **Context:** Mahjong scene. High tension, cheating, slapstick chaos.
- **Goal:** **MAXIMUM CHARACTER CONTRAST.** Avoid "same-voice" syndrome at all costs.

### 1. Formatting & Punctuation Rules (Strict)
- **English Punctuation Only:** Convert all Japanese punctuation to standard US English.
- **No Full-width Symbols:** No "…", no "！". Use "..." and "!".
- **Visualized SFX:** Translate the *sound/action*, not the spelling.
  - "ドカン" → "BOOOM!" (Visual) / "ぐす" → "*sniffle*" (Action).

### 2. Terminology & Glossary (Mahjong Trap Prevention)
- **"中" (Naka/Chun):** ALWAYS **"Red Dragon"** or **"Chun"**. (NEVER "middle/inside").
- **"ハコテン" (Hakoten):** **"Busted"** / **"Bankrupt"** / **"In the red"**.
- **"パイ" (Pai):** **"Tile"**.
- **"リーチ" (Riichi):** **"Riichi"** (or "Ready hand").

### 3. CHARACTER VOICE AMPLIFICATION (The "Anti-Bland" Protocol)
**Apply these specific stylistic constraints to widen the gap between characters.**

#### 両津 勘吉 (Ryotsu) — The "Roughneck" (Low Register)
* **Tone:** Loud, crude, greedy, uneducated but street-smart.
* **Grammar Constraint:** Use **Heavy Contractions** & **Slang**.
    * *Yes:* "Ain't," "Gonna," "Lemme," "Ya hear?"
    * *No:* "I am not," "Going to," "Please let me."
* **Vocabulary:** Working-class, visceral. Uses insults like "damn," "hell," "rotten."
* **Mahjong Vibe:** Aggressive bluffer.
* **Style Example:** "Heh, look at yer sorry faces! I'm gonna wipe ya clean!"

#### 中川 圭一 (Nakagawa) — The "Aristocrat" (High Register)
* **Tone:** Hyper-polite, shocked, logical, refined.
* **Grammar Constraint:** **Full Sentences** & **Minimal Contractions**.
    * *Yes:* "This is impossible," "I cannot believe it," "Senpai, please stop."
    * *No:* "It's no good," "Can't believe it," "Quit it."
* **Vocabulary:** Educated, precise, distinctively "posh" but panicked.
* **Mahjong Vibe:** The victim of Ryotsu's cheating; expresses shock through disbelief rather than anger.
* **Style Example:** "Senpai, surely you jest! That move is statistically impossible!"

#### 戸塚 (Totsuka) — The "Hard-Boiled" (Minimal Register)
* **Tone:** Gruff, masculine, impatient, intimidation-lite.
* **Grammar Constraint:** **Sentence Fragments** & **Dropped Pronouns**.
    * *Yes:* "Cheating bastard." "My turn." "Too slow."
    * *No:* "You are a cheating bastard." "It is my turn." "You are too slow."
* **Vocabulary:** Punchy, direct verbs. No fluff.
* **Mahjong Vibe:** Sharp observer. Catches the cheat instantly.
* **Style Example:** "Hey. That's a swap. Saw it with my own eyes."

### 4. Contrast Matrix (Reference for Tone Check)
*Scene: Reacting to a bad hand.*
* **Ryotsu:** "Crap! This hand's garbage!" (Explosive/Slang)
* **Nakagawa:** "This situation is... quite unfavorable." (Polite/Understated)
* **Totsuka:** "Dead hand." (Fragment/Direct)

### 5. Translation Execution
- **Brevity:** Keep bubbles tight (3-10 words).
- **Compression:** Prioritize the *character's flavor* over literal word-for-word translation.
